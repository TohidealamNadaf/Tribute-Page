A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The man who has inspired millions through his vision and wisdom

Here's a timeline of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
  • 1954 - Graduates in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli
  • 1960 - Completes Aerospace Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology
  • 1969 - Transfers to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  • 1980 - SLV-III successfully deploys the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit, making India an exclusive member of the Space Club
  • 1981 - Awarded the Padma Bhushan by the Government of India
  • 1992 - Appointed as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)
  • 1997 - Receives the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor
  • 1998 - Plays a pivotal role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests which established India as a nuclear weapon state
  • 2002 - Elected as the 11th President of India, serving until 2007. Known as the "People's President" for his accessibility to the common people
  • 2012 - Publishes his book "Turning Points: A journey through challenges," which outlines his vision for India
  • 2015 - Passes away on July 27 while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of scientific innovation and inspirational leadership

"A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's life story is an enduring example of the extraordinary influence that a single individual's vision, hard work, and dedication can have on shaping a nation's future." -- Ban Ki-moon, former Secretary-General of the United Nations
